For bright, warm herbal preparations: First extract the clear essence from roots using filtering cloth. Pour the filtered liquid into vessels, adding sweet herbs and clear preparations. The mixture should become bright and clear.

Heat the preparation gently while stirring. Pour into containers and let rest with medicinal applications. Add chalk clarifier if the liquid remains cloudy. The strong alcoholic solvent helps extract bitter compounds from the plant material.

For soaking treatments: Warm the preparation and pour over the affected area. Allow to drip and soak thoroughly. Remove any sediment by filtering through soft cotton material. The completed preparation should taste sweet when properly finished.

For bathing applications: Heat the liquid until warm but not hot. Immerse in cycles, allowing the medicine to penetrate. The bright, clear solution indicates proper extraction. Pour carefully to avoid disturbing settled material.

Signs of completion: The preparation becomes limpid and bright, with a sweet taste replacing initial bitterness. The filtered liquid should drip clearly without cloudiness. Store the finished medicine in covered vessels, away from light.

Apply by anointing the skin or through immersion baths. The warm preparation penetrates better than cold applications. Continue treatment cycles until the desired therapeutic effect is achieved. Properly prepared remedies maintain their potency and clarity over time.
